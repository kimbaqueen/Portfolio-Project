“What does success mean to you?”
There is a theme that I’ve heard when attending networking or personal development workshops. It is to take time and reflect – really dig down deep – and know who you are.
  • What is important to you?
  • What do you want to get out of life?
  • What motivates you?
  • What causes you to hesitate?
  • What do you ultimately want to accomplish?
  • What are you willing to compromise?
  • What is non-negotiable?
Understanding your goals and yourself is ultimately going to help you make more intentional decisions to get to where you want to be.
Life is different for everyone. Sometimes we are in survival mode, and make reactive decisions to get by. I get it. There is no wrong path to get to where you are going. But every reactive response can alter your path. Detours happen. It is up to us to pick up, re-evaluate the situation, and try again.
Try not to get discouraged or compare yourself to others success. The journey is different for us all. But it is up to us to keep pushing forward and don't lose sight of the big picture.
It takes a village. What I’ve found is if you reach out, support is everywhere.
Many want to help us succeed, but where do we start?
I’ll share more about where to find resources in your industry/community and how to grow your network. In the meantime, start within. Take a good inventory of the self-reflective questions above now, so that you are prepared to seize the next opportunity!
